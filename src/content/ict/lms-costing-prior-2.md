## 1. Purpose

This document recorded the operational costing baseline for YAT's on-premises Learning Management System (LMS) two operating years prior to the current baseline, and the common planning assumptions used by ICT at that time. It is retained alongside more recent baselines for trend-analysis and audit purposes.

### 3.2 Capital outlook — operating-model considerations

The LMS server was in **early operational life** at the time of this costing. The server had been deployed relatively recently following the LMS Replacement project (filed under Projects). No refresh was planned and no operating-model change was being considered.

The capital outlook in this baseline was therefore minimal:

| Item | Indicative cost |
|---|---:|
| Server warranty extension (optional) | $2,500 |
| Standard maintenance reserve | $1,500 |

## 4. Cost categorisation framework for change proposals

The same three cost-bearer categorisation applied to any change proposal (external consultant-priced, direct vendor / platform, YAT-internal). No material change proposals were active during this operating year.

## 5. Operational considerations and ICT priorities

The following operational factors were material at the time:

- **Avoided downtime.** Lifting LMS availability from the then-current baseline to the target 99.9% offered a modest avoided-downtime benefit; the cost-of-change vs benefit ratio did not at the time justify a major operating-model change.
- **Capacity for student growth.** The 12% growth trajectory was tracking comfortably within current capacity headroom.
- **Capacity for assessment-week peaks.** Then-current peaks handled without significant degradation.
- **Staff capability development.** YAT ICT staff capability development at the time focused on existing on-prem operations; cloud capability was not on the ICT learning roadmap.
- **Cloud migration.** Cloud migration was not part of YAT's strategic ICT planning during this operating year. The first reference to cloud migration in the ICT Strategic Plan appeared in the subsequent year's plan update.
