## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how YAT College manages its records and working documents: how they are classified, where and how they are filed, how versions are controlled, how long they are kept, and how they are securely disposed of. It ensures that documentation produced in the course of YAT's operations — including by external consultants and project teams engaged by YAT — is stored consistently, can be found and relied upon when needed, and meets YAT's legal and regulatory record-keeping obligations.

It works in conjunction with the Backup and Retention Policy (which sets retention periods and recovery arrangements) and the Privacy / Data Handling Policy (which governs personal information held within records).

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all YAT-held records and working documents, in all systems and across all business areas — the LMS, Application Services (Accounting / Office Administration), Active Directory, NAS file storage, the SharePoint document libraries, and any cloud-hosted YAT system (including the migrated LMS environment). It applies equally to YAT staff and to any external party engaged to deliver work for YAT, who must file their deliverables in accordance with this policy.

## 3. Records vs working documents

| Category | Definition | Management |
|---|---|---|
| **Record** | A document that evidences a decision, transaction, or obligation YAT must be able to produce later (e.g. an approved deployment report, a signed-off closure pack, a change-approval record, a financial or student record). | Formally managed: filed in the controlled location, version-baselined, retained for the period set in the Backup and Retention Policy, disposed of securely at end-of-retention. |
| **Working document** | A day-to-day draft, note, or in-progress file that supports work but is not itself the authoritative evidence. | Kept in the relevant project or team workspace until superseded; promoted to a record when finalised and approved. |

When a working document is finalised and approved, the approved version becomes a record and is filed and baselined accordingly; superseded drafts are not retained as records.

## 4. Filing and storage of documentation

All YAT documentation is saved and stored in YAT's controlled documentation locations — never solely on local workstations, personal drives, or email:

- **The YAT ICT Documentation Library** (SharePoint) holds policies, ICT operational documents, and system documentation. ICT records (architecture and design documents, deployment reports, configuration exports, test evidence, sign-off records) are filed here under the relevant system area.
- **Engagement / project workspaces** — each project has a dedicated workspace in the Documentation Library. Consultants and project teams engaged by YAT file their deliverables into that project's workspace as they are produced and at sign-off.
- **Business-area record stores** — Student Services, Finance, and HR file their records in their designated systems and libraries (e.g. the LMS / student management system for student records; the Accounting system for financial records).

Documentation that supports the implementation of an ICT change is filed in the relevant project workspace and cross-referenced from the change-approval record, in line with the ICT Change Management Procedure.

## 5. Naming conventions and version control

- **File naming** follows the pattern `<System>_<DocumentType>_<Version>_<YYYY-MM-DD>` — e.g. `LMS_DeploymentReport_v1.0_2026-03-14`. Names use the system or project identifier, a clear document type, the version, and the ISO date.
- **Version numbering** uses `major.minor`: draft revisions increment the minor number (`v0.1`, `v0.2`); the first approved/baselined version is `v1.0`; subsequent approved re-issues increment the major number.
- **A baselined record is not edited in place.** Changes are made by issuing a new version through the appropriate approval; the superseded version is retained read-only for the audit trail until end-of-retention.
- **One authoritative copy.** The controlled location holds the single source of truth; duplicates are avoided, and links are used in preference to copies.

## 6. Retention

Records are retained for the periods set out in the **Backup and Retention Policy**, which holds the per-category retention table (student records, assessment evidence, financial records, ICT operational logs, security and WHS records, and so on). This policy does not duplicate those periods; it governs *how* records are filed and version-controlled for the duration of their retention.

## 7. Disposal at end of retention

- Records that have reached the end of their retention period are securely destroyed, in line with the Backup and Retention Policy and, for personal information, Australian Privacy Principle 11.
- Disposal of a record is documented (what was disposed of, when, and on whose authority) so the disposal itself is auditable.
- Superseded working documents may be deleted once the approved record exists; records may only be disposed of at end-of-retention.

## 8. Audit and compliance

- Filing and version-control practice is reviewed as part of YAT's regular compliance activity, to confirm records are complete, correctly located, and retrievable.
- The policy supports YAT's obligations under the Standards for RTOs 2015 and the *Privacy Act 1988* (Cth).
- Material gaps (records missing, mis-filed, or not retrievable) are raised with the ICT Manager and, where personal information is affected, handled under the Security Baseline and Incident Response Policy.

## 9. Roles and responsibilities

- **ICT Manager:** owns this policy; maintains the ICT Documentation Library structure; ensures ICT records are filed and version-controlled.
- **Compliance Manager:** advises on record-keeping obligations and retention; oversees compliance review.
- **Records-holding business areas (Student Services, Finance, HR):** act as custodians for their record classes; ensure their records are filed and classified correctly.
- **Project and engagement teams (including engaged consultants):** file their deliverables in the correct project workspace, named and versioned per this policy, and ensure approved deliverables are baselined as records at sign-off.
- **All staff:** save working documents and records in the controlled locations, not on local or personal storage.

## 10. Policy ownership and review

- **Owner:** ICT Manager (with input from the Compliance Manager on retention and regulatory obligations).
- **Review cadence:** annually, or sooner if triggered by a material change in legislation, technology, or organisational structure.
- **Approval:** policy changes follow the ICT Change Management Procedure.
