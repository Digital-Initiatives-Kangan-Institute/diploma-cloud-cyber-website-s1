> The Records Management Policy will set out how YAT records and working documents are filed, version-controlled, and disposed of across the organisation.

## What this policy will cover

When authored, the policy will cover at minimum:

- **Records vs working documents** — classification (which artefacts are "records" requiring formal management vs day-to-day working files)
- **Filing locations** — the YAT ICT shared documentation repository, document management system / SharePoint, and what goes where
- **Naming conventions and version control** — file naming patterns, document version numbering, who can supersede a record
- **Retention periods** — cross-references the Backup and Retention Policy for the per-category retention table (student records, financial, ICT logs, WHS records)
- **Disposal procedures** — secure disposal at end-of-retention; chain of custody for deletion
- **Audit and compliance** — review cadence; demonstration of compliance with Standards for RTOs 2015 and the *Privacy Act 1988*
- **Roles and responsibilities** — owner of the policy, records custodians per business area

The policy provides the organisational policies and procedures backing for engagement deliverables that need to be filed post-engagement (deployment reports, closure packs, sign-off records).
