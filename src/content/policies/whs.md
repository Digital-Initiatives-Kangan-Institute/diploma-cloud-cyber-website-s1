## 1. Purpose

YAT College is committed to providing a safe and healthy workplace for all staff, students, contractors, and visitors. This policy sets out YAT's commitment to workplace health and safety and the framework by which that commitment is delivered.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all YAT premises (Cremorne campus), all activities carried out under YAT's direction, and all persons present at YAT's premises or undertaking work or study on YAT's behalf. It is consistent with the *Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004* (Vic) and related Victorian WorkSafe guidance.

## 3. YAT's commitments

YAT will, so far as is reasonably practicable:

- Provide and maintain a working environment that is safe and without risks to health.
- Provide and maintain plant, equipment, and systems of work that are safe and without risks to health.
- Provide such information, instruction, training, and supervision as is necessary to enable staff and students to perform their work and studies safely.
- Maintain workplaces in a safe and healthy condition.
- Provide adequate facilities (including amenities such as drinking water, lighting, and emergency access).
- Consult with staff and student representatives on health and safety matters that affect them.

## 4. Responsibilities

**All staff, students, contractors, and visitors** must:

- Take reasonable care for their own health and safety.
- Take reasonable care that their acts or omissions do not adversely affect the health and safety of others.
- Cooperate with YAT in relation to any action taken to comply with WHS legislation.
- Report hazards, incidents, near-misses, and injuries promptly (see §6).

**Managers and supervisors** must:

- Ensure their staff and students have appropriate training, supervision, and equipment to perform their roles safely.
- Address hazards within their area of control or escalate them appropriately.
- Investigate incidents and near-misses promptly and contribute to corrective action.

**The ICT Manager** is responsible for safety in the YAT server room and any ICT-specific risks (electrical safety, manual handling of equipment, computer-room access controls, UPS and fire-suppression systems). The server room is physically secured and air-conditioned and protected by UPS against power loss and electrical surges.

## 5. Workstation and ICT-related health

YAT supports staff and students using ICT equipment to work safely by:

- Providing ergonomic workstations and seating in staff offices and student labs.
- Offering ergonomic assessments on request via Student Services or HR.
- Reminding users to take regular screen-breaks; lab and office layouts include space for stand-up and rest breaks.

## 6. Hazard, incident, and near-miss reporting

Any hazard, incident, near-miss, or injury must be reported as soon as practicable:

- **Immediate physical hazards or injuries:** alert the nearest first-aid officer and call emergency services if required.
- **Ongoing hazards or near-misses:** report via the WHS reporting channel within 24 hours.
- **ICT-specific hazards (e.g. faulty equipment, electrical concerns, server-room access issues):** report to the ICT Service Desk; serious or recurring matters are escalated to the ICT Manager.

Reports are investigated by the relevant manager. Outcomes (root cause + corrective action) are recorded and shared with the affected area.

## 7. Emergency procedures

YAT maintains a campus emergency plan covering fire, medical emergency, and evacuation. All staff and students are inducted into the plan on commencement. Evacuation drills are conducted annually. The plan and assembly point details are posted in classrooms, offices, and shared areas.

## 8. Training

WHS induction is provided to all new staff and students. Role-specific training is provided where work involves elevated risk (e.g. lab work, server-room access, manual handling).

## 9. Policy ownership and review

- **Owner:** WHS Coordinator (with the CEO accountable at executive level).
- **Review cadence:** every 2 years, or sooner if triggered by a material incident, change in legislation, or change in operations.
- **Approval:** policy changes follow YAT's governance processes; ICT-specific updates also follow the ICT Change Management Procedure.
