## 1. Purpose and scope

This policy defines the acceptable and unacceptable use of YAT College's information and communications technology (ICT) resources — including networks, servers, end-user devices, the LMS, email, cloud services, and any account or credential issued by YAT. It applies to all staff, students, contractors, external consultants (including MP Tech Solutions), and any other party with access to YAT ICT resources.

This policy works in conjunction with the User Access Policy, the WHS Policy, and the Privacy / Data Handling Policy.

## 2. Acceptable use

YAT ICT resources are provided to support teaching, learning, administration, and authorised business activities. Acceptable use includes:

- Using YAT systems to undertake duties or studies for which access has been granted.
- Storing and processing YAT-related work products in approved systems.
- Reasonable, occasional personal use that does not interfere with work or study, does not consume material network or storage resources, and does not breach any other clause of this policy.

## 3. Unacceptable use

The following activities are prohibited on YAT ICT resources:

- Accessing, creating, storing, or distributing material that is unlawful, discriminatory, harassing, defamatory, obscene, or otherwise inappropriate to a workplace and educational setting.
- Sharing account credentials with any other person, or using another person's credentials.
- Bypassing or attempting to bypass YAT's security controls, monitoring, or access restrictions.
- Installing unlicensed or unauthorised software on YAT-managed devices.
- Using YAT resources for personal commercial gain or any non-YAT-sanctioned business activity.
- Storing personal cryptocurrency mining, gaming, or media-streaming workloads on YAT systems.
- Connecting unauthorised devices to YAT's internal network zones.
- Knowingly introducing malware or destructive code, or interfering with the integrity of YAT systems.
- Using YAT email or messaging for spam, chain mail, or unsolicited advertising.
- Accessing or attempting to access data, systems, or accounts beyond the scope of one's role.

## 4. Confidentiality and data handling

- YAT-held personal information (student records, staff records) must be handled in accordance with the Privacy / Data Handling Policy and the *Privacy Act 1988* (Cth).
- Confidential information must not be removed from YAT systems or shared with external parties except as authorised by the relevant manager and in accordance with the change-management or contract framework.

## 5. Monitoring

- YAT monitors use of its ICT resources for security, performance, compliance, and incident investigation purposes.
- Monitoring is conducted lawfully and in line with YAT's Privacy Policy. Users should have no expectation of privacy for activity on YAT systems beyond what that policy allows.
- Routine monitoring includes authentication logs, access logs, network flow records, and email/web filtering. Targeted monitoring of an individual requires authorisation by the ICT Manager and, where applicable, HR.

## 6. Reporting concerns

Users who become aware of any potential breach of this policy — by themselves, another user, or an external party — must report it promptly to:

- their immediate supervisor (for staff)
- their trainer or Program Leader (for students)
- the ICT Service Desk (for technical security concerns)
- the ICT Manager (for serious or sensitive matters)

## 7. Sanctions

Breach of this policy may result in:

- Loss of access to YAT ICT resources (per the User Access Policy §11 — Sanctions).
- Formal disciplinary action under the relevant employment or student conduct policy.
- Referral to law enforcement where conduct constitutes a criminal offence.

## 8. Policy ownership and review

- **Owner:** ICT Manager (in consultation with HR for staff-related provisions and Student Services for student-related provisions).
- **Review cadence:** annually, or sooner if triggered by a material change in technology, legislation, or organisational structure.
- **Approval:** policy changes follow the ICT Change Management Procedure.
