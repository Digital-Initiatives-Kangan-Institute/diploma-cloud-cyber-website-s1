## 1. Purpose and scope

This policy defines how access to YAT College's information systems, networks, and applications is granted, used, reviewed, and revoked. It applies to all staff, students, contractors, external consultants (including MP Tech Solutions), and any other party with an account in any YAT system.

This policy supports YAT's compliance obligations under the *Privacy Act 1988* (Cth), the Australian Privacy Principles, and the *Standards for Registered Training Organisations 2015*. It is aligned to the Australian Cyber Security Centre's Essential Eight mitigation strategies.

## 2. Definitions

- **Account** — any identifier issued by YAT that grants access to one or more YAT systems.
- **Privileged account** — any account with administrative rights to any system component (server, application, database, network device, identity directory).
- **Standard account** — any account that is not a privileged account.
- **Identity directory** — YAT's Active Directory (AD) 2016 deployment, which is the authoritative source for user identity.

## 3. Role-based access

YAT's environment is structured as follows:

- The network is split into **two security zones**: a student zone and a staff zone.
- **Students** have access only to resources in the student zone and to the LMS application.
- Access to network objects in both zones is controlled via **Active Directory permissions**.
- A **single VPN server** provides remote access for **staff only**.
- All centrally-managed campus computers are AD-joined and run Windows 10 Enterprise.

## 4. Role groups and permissions matrix

| Role group | AD group | Zone access | LMS access | Application Services access | Privileged systems access | Remote access |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Students** | `YAT-Students` | Student zone only | LMS — learner role (enrol, view, submit) | None | None | None |
| **Trainers / Assessors** | `YAT-Trainers` | Staff zone + student zone (read where required) | LMS — teacher role (author, grade, manage attendance) | Read-only (where role requires) | None | VPN |
| **Program Leaders** | `YAT-ProgramLeaders` | Staff zone + student zone | LMS — admin role (course structure, assessment moderation) | Read where required | None | VPN |
| **Administrative staff** | `YAT-Admin` | Staff zone | LMS — read for support purposes | Read/write per function (Finance, Student Services, HR) | None | VPN |
| **ICT staff (operators)** | `YAT-ICTOps` | Both zones | LMS — admin | All servers (operator-level) | Yes (named privileged accounts) | VPN |
| **ICT Manager** | `YAT-ICTMgr` | Both zones | LMS — admin | All systems | Yes (full privileged access, change approval authority) | VPN |
| **External consultants (e.g. MTS)** | `YAT-Contractor-<engagement>` | Per engagement scope (least-privilege) | Per engagement scope | Per engagement scope | Per engagement scope; named privileged accounts only where required by SOW | VPN, time-bounded |
| **Service accounts** | `YAT-Svc-<service>` | Per service | Per service | Per service | Per service | None (non-interactive) |

## 5. Account lifecycle (joiner / mover / leaver)

**Joiner:**
1. Hiring manager or sponsor (for contractors/students) raises an account-request ticket including: full name, role, start date, AD group(s) required, and (for contractors) engagement end date.
2. Approval by relevant department head (for staff), Student Services (for students), or ICT Manager (for contractors).
3. ICT provisions the account in AD with the approved group memberships. Initial password is generated by ICT and provided to the user via secure channel; user must change on first sign-in.
4. For privileged access, a separate named privileged account is provisioned (see §7) — privileges are not added to the user's standard account.

**Mover (role change within YAT):**
1. New role's manager raises a move ticket specifying the new role's required AD group memberships.
2. ICT removes the user from previous role-specific groups and adds them to new role groups within 5 business days of the role change.
3. Any system-specific access entitlements not driven by AD groups are reviewed and adjusted.

**Leaver:**
1. HR (or sponsor for contractors) notifies ICT of the leaver date at least 5 business days in advance.
2. On the leaver date, ICT disables the user's accounts at end of business and removes group memberships.
3. Accounts remain disabled for 90 days then are deleted. Mailbox, home drive and any LMS authoring content are retained per the Backup and Retention Policy.
4. Privileged accounts owned by the leaver are disabled immediately on notification (not at end-of-business).

## 6. Authentication and passwords

- **Identity directory:** all interactive sign-ins authenticate via AD.
- **Password length:** minimum 14 characters.
- **Password complexity:** AD complexity rules enforced; passphrases encouraged over complex short passwords.
- **Password rotation:** standard accounts — no scheduled rotation (rotation only on suspected compromise, per ACSC current guidance). Privileged accounts — rotated every 90 days.
- **Password history:** last 24 passwords retained; reuse prohibited.
- **Failed sign-in lockout:** 10 failed attempts within 15 minutes triggers a 30-minute lockout. Repeat lockouts within 24 hours trigger ICT review of the account.
- **Shared accounts:** prohibited. Service accounts are not shared accounts and are used only for documented non-interactive purposes.

## 7. Multi-factor authentication (MFA)

MFA is required for:
- All privileged account sign-ins (without exception)
- All remote access via VPN
- All access to the LMS by staff with grading or course-management permissions (Trainers, Program Leaders, ICT Admin role)
- All access to the Application Services system (Accounting / Office Admin) by Administrative staff
- Any access to YAT systems from an unmanaged device (subject to §9)

MFA is not currently required for student LMS access or staff access from on-campus AD-joined devices to in-zone resources, but is under review.

## 8. Privileged access management (PAM)

- **Separation:** every user with privileged access has both a standard account (for day-to-day work — email, documents, browsing) and a separately-named privileged account (for administrative tasks only).
- **Naming convention:** privileged accounts are named `adm-<surname>` or `svc-<purpose>`.
- **Use of privilege:** privileged accounts are used only for tasks that require privilege. Browsing, email, and routine work are prohibited from privileged sessions.
- **Just-in-time elevation:** where supported by the system (e.g. RunAs on Windows servers), privileged credentials are entered only at the point of need, not held in a persistent session.
- **Audit:** privileged account use is logged centrally and reviewed quarterly by the ICT Manager.

## 9. Remote access

- **VPN-only:** staff remote access to YAT internal systems is permitted only via the YAT VPN. Direct exposure of internal services to the Internet is prohibited.
- **Device state:** remote access from a YAT-managed device is preferred. Access from a personal/unmanaged device is permitted only with MFA and is restricted to non-privileged accounts.
- **Geographic restrictions:** none in standard policy. Travel-out-of-country sign-ins generate an ICT alert for review.
- **Contractor remote access:** time-bounded to the engagement end date in the contract; revoked immediately at engagement end regardless of leaver-cycle timing.

## 10. Access reviews

- **Quarterly:** ICT Manager reviews all privileged account memberships. Discrepancies are remediated within 10 business days.
- **Annually:** all role groups reviewed by the relevant department head against current organisational structure. Sign-off captured and filed per the Change Management Procedure.
- **Event-driven:** access reviewed at any role change (mover), security incident, or material organisational change.

## 11. Sanctions

- **Standard breach** (e.g. sharing a password, leaving a privileged session unattended): formal warning by manager + ICT-led refresher.
- **Material breach** (e.g. accessing data unrelated to role, granting access without approval): formal warning escalating to suspension of access; managed under the relevant employment or student conduct policy.
- **Severe breach** (e.g. deliberate unauthorised access, data exfiltration, account sharing for impersonation): immediate account suspension, formal disciplinary process, referral to law enforcement if applicable.

## 12. Policy ownership and review

- **Owner:** ICT Manager.
- **Review cadence:** annually, or sooner if triggered by a material change in technology, organisational structure, or compliance landscape.
- **Approval:** policy changes follow the ICT Change Management Procedure.
