## 1. Purpose

YAT College is committed to protecting the personal information it collects, holds, uses, and discloses. This policy sets out how YAT handles personal information in compliance with the *Privacy Act 1988* (Cth), the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs), the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme, and the *Standards for Registered Training Organisations (RTOs) 2015*.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all personal information YAT holds about students, staff, contractors, prospective students, and other individuals interacting with YAT. It applies to all forms in which that information is held — electronic, paper, and otherwise — and to all staff, students, contractors, and external service providers (including MP Tech Solutions) who handle YAT-held personal information.

## 3. What personal information YAT collects

**Student personal information** may include: full name, date of birth, contact details, USI (Unique Student Identifier), enrolment details, prior education and qualifications, fee status, attendance records, assessment results and submissions, learning support needs, and any other information necessary for course administration and statutory reporting to ASQA / DET.

**Staff personal information** may include: full name, contact details, qualifications, employment history, tax and superannuation details, performance records, and other information necessary for employment.

**Contractor / consultant personal information** may include: name, contact details, qualifications, and engagement scope.

## 4. How personal information is collected

YAT collects personal information directly from the individual where reasonable and practicable — through application forms, enrolment processes, HR onboarding, and direct interactions. Where collection from a third party is necessary (e.g. prior-education verification), the individual is informed.

## 5. How personal information is used and disclosed

Personal information is used only for the purposes for which it was collected, or for related purposes the individual would reasonably expect. Typical uses include:

- Course administration (enrolment, attendance, assessment, completion)
- Student support and welfare
- Statutory reporting to ASQA and DET (AVETMISS reporting)
- Employment administration (for staff)
- Compliance with legal obligations

Personal information is not disclosed to third parties except where required by law, with the individual's consent, or to engaged service providers operating under contractual confidentiality obligations (e.g. MTS for ICT support).

## 6. Storage and security

Personal information is stored in YAT's ICT systems (primarily the LMS, the Application Services system, and the staff-zone NAS). Access is controlled per the User Access Policy. Security controls include role-based access, multi-factor authentication for staff accessing sensitive systems, audit logging, encrypted storage where supported, and secure backups per the Backup and Retention Policy.

## 7. Cross-border disclosure (APP 8)

YAT's working assumption is that **personal information of YAT students must remain stored within Australia** to support compliance with APP 8 and the *Privacy Act 1988*. Cloud services used to host YAT personal information must be configured to use Australian data-residency regions (e.g. AWS `ap-southeast-2` Sydney for the migrated LMS). Engagement of any overseas-hosted service requires:

- Privacy impact assessment
- ICT Manager and senior management approval
- Contractual safeguards binding the provider to APP-equivalent obligations

## 8. Access and correction

Individuals have the right to request access to the personal information YAT holds about them, and to request correction of inaccurate information. Requests should be made in writing to the YAT Privacy contact. YAT will respond within 30 days.

## 9. Complaints and breach handling

Complaints about YAT's handling of personal information can be made to the YAT Privacy contact. If not satisfied with YAT's response, an individual may complain to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).

Suspected data breaches must be reported immediately to the ICT Manager. YAT follows the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme: where a data breach is likely to result in serious harm, affected individuals and the OAIC will be notified as required by the *Privacy Act 1988*. The incident-response process is described in the Security Baseline and Incident Response policy.

## 10. Policy ownership and review

- **Owner:** ICT Manager (jointly with HR for staff personal information and Student Services for student personal information; ultimately accountable to the CEO).
- **Review cadence:** annually, or sooner if triggered by a material change in legislation, technology, or organisational structure.
- **Approval:** policy changes follow the ICT Change Management Procedure.
