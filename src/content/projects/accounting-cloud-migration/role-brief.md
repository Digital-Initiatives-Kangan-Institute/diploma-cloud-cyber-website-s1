This brief sets out the roles, responsibilities, and reporting relationships for the **MTS Accounting System Cloud Migration** engagement — the evaluation of whether YAT's Accounting & Office Administration system (Ledgerline) should be renewed on-premises or migrated to the cloud. The Master Services Agreement is the authoritative source for engagement scope and governance; this brief is the operational reference held by each role during day-to-day work.

## Engagement context

YAT College has engaged **MTS Consulting** to evaluate the future operating model for its Accounting & Office Administration system. The Application Services server hosting Ledgerline is approaching end of warranty and capacity headroom, and YAT's ICT Strategic Plan sets a direction of reducing dependency on in-house server infrastructure. MTS has been asked to analyse the options and produce a board-ready Business Case recommending a way forward.

This engagement is the **analysis and planning phase** for this system — the same shape of work MTS performs at the front of any migration: strategic analysis, cost-benefit analysis, prioritised action plan, and a Business Case presented for approval.

**How MTS staffs YAT's engagements.** MTS runs several engagements with YAT as part of YAT's cloud-transition program, and **assigns its consultants across those engagements as work demands** — the same consultant may pick up the Accounting System evaluation, the LMS migration, or another YAT engagement as the program progresses. You work as an MTS consultant across YAT's engagements; the role, reporting lines, and standards are the same on each.

## Scope of the MTS consulting engagement

**In scope for MTS (this engagement):**

- Strategic alignment analysis, current-state review, and gap analysis for the Accounting system
- Options analysis (renew on-prem vs migrate to AWS), cost-benefit analysis, risk assessment
- Prioritised action plan and board-ready Business Case + presentation

**Out of scope for MTS — retained by YAT in-house teams (relevant to later phases):**

- Ledgerline application installation and configuration on any new infrastructure
- Migration of the SQL Server database and financial data
- Cutover, parallel running, and decommissioning
- Organisational change management and finance-team training

*This mirrors the YAT/MTS scope split on YAT's other engagements: MTS leads the cloud-infrastructure analysis and (in later phases) build; YAT in-house IT retains application install, data migration, cutover, and change management.*
