> **Engagement closed 16 December 2022.** Retained on the YAT intranet for reference and audit per the YAT Records Management Policy.

## Context

The following functional and non-functional requirements for the YAT LMS Replacement engagement were established by YAT ICT in early 2022 prior to the engagement of AccentLoitte. They set the targets the engagement was delivered against and were referenced throughout LMS selection, deployment, data migration, and Cutover activities. The Consultation Notes filed against this project record the consultations these requirements were derived from.

## Requirements

The Replacement LMS solution had to:

- **Replace the legacy GrayBoard LMS with a contemporary commercial LMS platform.** GrayBoard had reached end-of-supportable-life. The Replacement LMS was required to be a current commercial product with active vendor support, an active user community, and a credible long-term product roadmap.

- **Deploy on-premises at the YAT Cremorne campus.** Cloud hosting was noted as a future consideration but was not part of this engagement's scope. The Replacement LMS had to be installable and operable on YAT-managed on-premises infrastructure consistent with YAT's then-current ICT operating model.

- **Run on Windows Server 2016 application hosting standard.** The Replacement LMS had to be compatible with YAT's then-current server operating system standard for on-prem application hosting.

- **Preserve all student records from GrayBoard with full data integrity.** Long-tail student-record retention obligations under the *Standards for Registered Training Organisations 2015* required that no records be lost in the migration. Post-migration record counts had to reconcile against pre-migration counts; sample records had to verify correctly for content fidelity.

- **Achieve baseline availability of at least 99%.** The Replacement LMS had to deliver a meaningful step up from the legacy GrayBoard's effective availability (which had been degrading prior to replacement).

- **Cutover during the December–January teaching break.** To minimise disruption to active teaching, Cutover had to occur during the late-December to mid-January period when scheduled teaching is minimal.

- **Trainer and staff training delivered before Cutover.** All teaching and administrative staff who interact with the LMS had to receive role-appropriate training before Cutover so the post-Cutover support burden could be contained.

- **Integrate with existing YAT systems.** Required integrations:
  - Active Directory authentication (single sign-on for staff and students)
  - Office 365 SMTP for email notifications generated by the LMS
  - AVETMISS / ASQA statutory reporting export capability
  - YAT-internal LDAP-based user-group structures (per the User Access Policy in force at the time)

- **Meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility.** The Replacement LMS had to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.1 at Level AA to support YAT's obligations under the *Disability Discrimination Act 1992* (Cth).

- **Performance baseline match or exceed GrayBoard.** Typical-load response times had to be no worse than GrayBoard's measured performance pre-replacement, and ideally improved. The peak-load profile (~3× concurrent users during the last two weeks of each term, per existing YAT LMS load history) had to be supported without significant degradation.

- **Vendor support tier appropriate to criticality.** The Replacement LMS vendor support contract had to include severity-1 incident response within 4 hours during YAT teaching hours and 8 hours outside teaching hours. (This requirement was set at a lower tier than the post-migration cloud-era target of 1 hour, reflecting the then-current expectations for on-prem LMS hosting.)
