## 1. Parties

**Client:** **YAT College Pty Ltd** (ABN 12 345 678 901), a Registered Training Organisation, of 175 Cremorne Street, Cremorne VIC 3121, Australia (**YAT**).

**Consultant:** **AccentLoitte Pty Ltd** (ABN 47 583 921 064), of Level 32, 525 Collins Street, Melbourne VIC 3000, Australia (**AccentLoitte** or **the Consultant**).

YAT and AccentLoitte are referred to collectively as the **Parties** and individually as a **Party**.

## 2. Background

A. YAT operated an in-house Learning Management System (the **legacy LMS**, internally known as **GrayBoard**, originally developed in or around 2010 on a Java / JSP technology stack) deployed at the YAT Cremorne campus.

B. The legacy LMS was approaching end-of-supportable-life. Its technology stack was no longer aligned to industry standards for educational platforms, it required disproportionate maintenance effort, and it could not support YAT's growth trajectory or its strategic requirement to broaden online learning delivery.

C. YAT determined to procure and deploy a contemporary commercial Learning Management System to replace the legacy LMS, with end-to-end delivery (selection, on-premises deployment, data migration, cutover, user training, and handover) by an external consultancy.

D. YAT selected AccentLoitte to lead the LMS Replacement project under this Agreement.

E. The Parties wish to record the terms on which AccentLoitte will provide the Services to YAT.

## 4. Services and scope of work

### 4.1 Engagement structure

AccentLoitte will provide the Services in three sequential Phases:

| Phase | Title | Scope summary |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Selection and Implementation Plan | Define functional and non-functional requirements for the Replacement LMS; evaluate market options; recommend a Replacement LMS to the YAT executive; produce an implementation plan including deployment design, data migration approach, cutover strategy, and training plan. |
| Phase 2 | Deployment and Data Migration | Procure and provision on-premises infrastructure for the Replacement LMS at the YAT Cremorne campus; install and configure the selected Replacement LMS; extract YAT data from the legacy LMS; transform and load YAT data into the Replacement LMS; conduct parallel-running validation; execute Cutover. |
| Phase 3 | Stabilisation and Closure | Provide post-Cutover support during the stabilisation period; deliver user training to YAT staff and trainers; produce as-built documentation and operational runbooks; conduct knowledge transfer to YAT ICT; produce the project Closure Pack and obtain closure sign-off under YAT's Change Management Procedure. |

### 4.2 Detailed scope

Detailed scope for each Phase is set out in the supporting project documents produced during the Engagement (now archived with this Agreement in the YAT intranet's project record).

AccentLoitte performed the Services with reasonable skill and care, by appropriately qualified personnel, and in compliance with YAT's documented policies and procedures referenced in clauses 9 and 12 of this Agreement.

## 5. Out of scope

The following work is **not** included in this Engagement and remained the responsibility of YAT in-house ICT and Administration teams during the Engagement, or of separately-engaged third parties as noted:

- Hardware procurement decisions outside the recommendation made in Phase 1 (procured by YAT directly through its existing hardware-supplier relationships);
- Networking and firewall changes outside the LMS infrastructure boundary (managed by YAT ICT);
- Active Directory account provisioning beyond the LMS service accounts (managed by YAT ICT);
- Ongoing operational support of the Replacement LMS after acceptance of the Phase 3 Closure Pack (separately contracted with a different MTS-supplied support engagement);
- All YAT-side communications to external stakeholders (managed by YAT Marketing); and
- All YAT-side end-user support after the stabilisation period in Phase 3.

## 7. Term, suspension, and termination

### 7.1 Term

This Agreement commenced on the Effective Date and continued until Acceptance of Deliverable D3.4 (Phase 3 Closure Pack), which occurred on 16 December 2022.

### 7.2 Suspension

YAT was entitled to suspend the Engagement at any time by written notice to AccentLoitte. No suspensions were invoked during the Engagement.

### 7.3 Termination for convenience

YAT was entitled to terminate this Agreement by giving AccentLoitte not less than 20 Business Days' written notice. This right was not invoked.

### 7.4 Termination for cause

Either Party was entitled to terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other Party committed a material breach (with the standard 10-Business-Day remedy period for breaches capable of remedy) or became insolvent. No termination for cause occurred.

### 7.5 Effect of termination

Clauses 12 (Confidentiality), 13 (Intellectual Property), and 14 (Warranties, Liability, Insurance) survive the closure of this Agreement.

## 8. Acceptance

### 8.1 Submission

AccentLoitte submitted each Deliverable to the YAT ICT Manager for review with all evidence specified in clause 6 for that Deliverable.

### 8.2 Review period

YAT reviewed each submitted Deliverable within **5 Business Days** of submission and either accepted it in writing or rejected it in writing, specifying the reasons by reference to the acceptance evidence requirements in clause 6.

### 8.3 Re-submission

Where a Deliverable was rejected, AccentLoitte remediated and re-submitted within a reasonable period agreed with YAT. The review cycle in clause 8.2 applied to each re-submission.

The acceptance record for this Engagement shows three Deliverables required re-submission (D2.3 — data migration; D3.2 — user training; D3.3 — operational runbooks). All re-submissions were Accepted within agreed timeframes.

### 8.4 Phase progression

AccentLoitte did not commence Deliverables in a subsequent Phase until all Deliverables in the prior Phase had been Accepted.

## 9. Change control

### 9.1 Change Management Procedure

Substantive changes to the scope, Deliverables, fees, or timing of the Services were governed by:

(a) the YAT Change Management Procedure on the YAT intranet (incorporated by reference); and

(b) the Change Request process in this clause 9.

### 9.2 Change Request process

A Change Request set out:

- A description of the proposed change;
- The reason for the change;
- The impact on scope, Deliverables, fees, timing, and risk;
- Any associated rollout, rollback, or transition arrangements; and
- The proposed approval authority.

## 12. Confidentiality and data protection

### 12.1 Confidentiality

Each Party agreed to:

(a) hold the other Party's Confidential Information in confidence;

(b) use Confidential Information only for the purposes of this Engagement; and

(c) not disclose Confidential Information except to its own personnel, advisers, or sub-contractors who had a legitimate need to know and were bound by equivalent obligations of confidentiality.

### 12.2 Personal information

AccentLoitte handled YAT-held personal information in accordance with:

(a) the *Privacy Act 1988* (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles; and

(b) the YAT Privacy / Data Handling Policy (incorporated by reference).

A Privacy Impact Assessment was conducted prior to data migration in Phase 2 and is held with the Closure Pack.

### 12.3 Data residency

YAT data was processed and stored within Australia throughout the Engagement. No overseas-hosted services were used.

### 12.4 Data return or destruction

On closure of this Agreement, AccentLoitte certified that all YAT Confidential Information in its possession had been securely destroyed. Destruction certificates are held with the Closure Pack.

## 13. Intellectual property

### 13.1 Pre-existing IP

Each Party retained ownership of intellectual property it owned before the Effective Date or developed independently of the Engagement (**Pre-existing IP**).

### 13.2 New IP

Intellectual property created specifically for YAT under this Agreement (**New IP**) was assigned to YAT on payment of the relevant Phase fee. New IP includes the as-deployed LMS environment configuration, environment-specific scripts and templates, and Deliverable documents.

### 13.3 Background tooling

Generic tooling, scripts, libraries, accelerators, methodologies, and architectural patterns used by AccentLoitte across multiple engagements (**Background Tooling**) remained the Pre-existing IP of AccentLoitte. AccentLoitte granted YAT a perpetual, royalty-free, non-exclusive licence to use Background Tooling incorporated into any Deliverable to the extent necessary for YAT's use of that Deliverable.

### 13.4 No third-party IP infringement

AccentLoitte warranted that the Deliverables would not infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party. No infringement claims have been received.

## 14. Warranties, liability, and insurance

### 14.1 Warranties

AccentLoitte warranted that:

(a) the Services would be performed with reasonable skill and care, by appropriately qualified personnel;

(b) AccentLoitte held all licences, registrations, and authorisations required for the performance of the Services; and

(c) AccentLoitte would comply with all laws and regulations applicable to the performance of the Services.

### 14.2 Insurance

AccentLoitte maintained throughout the Engagement, with reputable insurers:

(a) professional indemnity insurance with a limit of indemnity of $10,000,000 per claim;

(b) public liability insurance with a limit of indemnity of $20,000,000 per occurrence; and

(c) workers' compensation insurance as required by law.

### 14.3 Liability cap

The total aggregate liability of each Party under or in connection with this Agreement was limited to twice the aggregate fees paid by YAT under this Agreement, with the standard carve-outs for confidentiality breach, IP infringement, wilful default, and personal injury.

### 14.4 Exclusion of indirect loss

Neither Party was liable to the other for any indirect, consequential, special, or punitive loss.
