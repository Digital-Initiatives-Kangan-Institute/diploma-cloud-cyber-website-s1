## How to use this list

When a design decision or compliance review touches on "legislative requirements" or "compliance obligations", reference the items below by name and identify which one applies. Where a YAT policy (Privacy Policy, Backup and Retention Policy, etc.) already operationalises a legislative requirement, cite both the policy and the underlying legislation.

This list is a starting point, not a complete compliance map. Specialised work (e.g. clinical health data, financial services, defence) introduces additional regimes not in scope here. Legal interpretation is the role of YAT Compliance and external counsel.

## Privacy and data protection

- **Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)** — federal privacy legislation; sets the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) framework. YAT, as an organisation holding student personal information, is bound by the APPs. Operationalised at YAT in the Privacy / Data Handling Policy.
- **Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)** — 13 principles inside the Privacy Act covering collection, use, disclosure, quality, security, access, and cross-border transfer. APP 8 (cross-border disclosure) underpins YAT's Australian-data-residency requirement for the migrated LMS.
- **Notifiable Data Breaches scheme (Privacy Act Part IIIC)** — mandatory notification to affected individuals and the OAIC where an eligible data breach is likely to result in serious harm. Operationalised in the Security Baseline and Incident Response policy.

## RTO regulation

- **National Vocational Education and Training Regulator Act 2011 (Cth)** — established ASQA (Australian Skills Quality Authority) as the national regulator for VET.
- **Standards for Registered Training Organisations (RTOs) 2015** — the substantive ASQA standards YAT operates under. Sets requirements around training and assessment, learner support, governance, records and reporting. Drives the long student-records retention periods in the Backup and Retention Policy.
- **AVETMISS data reporting** — statutory data reporting to ASQA / DET via the Total VET Activity (TVA) reporting framework.

## Workplace health and safety

- **Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004 (Vic)** — Victorian WHS legislation; YAT's primary workplace safety framework. Operationalised at YAT in the WHS Policy.
- **Occupational Health and Safety Regulations 2017 (Vic)** — subordinate regulations under the OHS Act.

## Anti-discrimination and accessibility

- **Disability Discrimination Act 1992 (Cth)** — federal anti-discrimination law; relevant to accessibility of YAT's services, including digital services (the LMS). Drives the WCAG 2.1 AA requirement for the LMS.
- **Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (Vic)** — Victorian anti-discrimination law; covers employment and provision of services.

## Employment and financial records

- **Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)** — federal employment legislation; sets record-keeping obligations that drive the 7-year retention for staff employment records in the Backup and Retention Policy.
- **Income Tax Assessment Act 1936 (Cth) — record-keeping provisions** — drives the 7-year retention for financial records.
- **Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992 (Cth)** — superannuation record-keeping obligations for staff.

## Online safety and student protection

- **Online Safety Act 2021 (Cth)** — federal online-safety framework; relevant to YAT's responsibilities for online learning environments including the LMS.
- **Child Wellbeing and Safety Act 2005 (Vic)** — Victorian child-safety framework; relevant to YAT students under 18.

## Intellectual property

- **Copyright Act 1968 (Cth)** — federal copyright law. Relevant to YAT's use and distribution of course materials (statutory educational licence under Part VB applies to RTOs).

## Cyber security and critical infrastructure

- **Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018 (Cth)** — federal critical-infrastructure framework. Not currently applicable to private RTOs but worth tracking; relevant if YAT's footprint or function changes materially.

## Notes

- Citations above are by short title only. Full Commonwealth Acts are available at [legislation.gov.au](https://www.legislation.gov.au); Victorian Acts at [legislation.vic.gov.au](https://www.legislation.vic.gov.au).
- Legislation versions referenced are current as at the time of writing; always confirm the in-force version when making a real compliance call.
