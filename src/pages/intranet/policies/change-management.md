Change management must follow the company processes and policies.

For all changes to production systems, a risk assessment must be prepared and approved by the ICT manager. Depending on the risk impact, sign-off by senior management and affected business units may need to be obtained.

The ICT change management process is listed below:

1. Complete the change request form:
   - Description of change and provide reason/justification for the change
   - Affected systems
   - Risks and impact of making the change
   - Duration of applying the change and potential system unavailability while the change is applied
   - Rollout plan
   - Backout plan (aka rollback plan)
   - Overall duration
   - Proposed date/time to apply the change
   - Person responsible for implementing the change
2. If required, produce a risk and impact analysis and attach the document to the change request
3. Present relevant change request documentation to the ICT manager for approval
4. Sign off by ICT manager and if required by senior management and affected business unit heads
5. Apply the change to the environment
6. Confirm the change achieved the intended outcome without adversely affecting the environment. If required, restore the initial environment.
7. Notify relevant parties of the outcome
8. Debrief and go over lessons learned
